1The big picture

You have a brilliant service and a website that hides it.

That gap is the biggest opportunity in the business.

  • ✓
    Where you are todayA trusted, expert service built over 35 years. But the website makes you look like a small directory, not the specialists you are.
  • ✓
    Where you could beAustralia's clear leader in conference and venue sourcing. The same great service, finally shown the way it deserves.
  • ✓
    What it takesLaunch the new website, let technology handle the busywork, and turn 35 years of know-how into something the whole team can use.
In one line

This is not a new website. It is a plan to make what you already do brilliantly visible to the right people.

2The old website

It loses people before you ever speak to them.

Three problems are costing you enquiries.

  • 1
    It leads with "free"The biggest word on the page makes a premium service look cheap. It attracts the wrong enquiries and puts off the right ones.
  • 2
    It offers no proofNo client names, no results, no faces. Visitors have no reason to trust it, so they leave.
  • 3
    It gives no clear next stepSomeone ready to enquire has nowhere obvious to go, so the interest is simply lost.
Why it matters

The website is the first thing a client meets, before any of you. Right now it is your weakest salesperson.

3The new website

It does the selling the old one never could.

The new design already gets the important things right.

  • ✓
    It sells your real value"We return three venues, and we've stood in all of them." It puts your relationships and expertise front and centre.
  • ✓
    It proves you can be trustedReal client names, real quotes, and your own faces. Trust is shown, not just claimed.
  • ✓
    It makes enquiring easyA short brief sits right at the top. People can reach you in 30 seconds, while they are most interested.

One thing to add: a few real numbers. Dollars saved, hours returned, events delivered. Proof in figures will make it even harder to ignore.

In five seconds

The old site says "a small operator." The new one says "the trusted experts." That shift happens on every visit, for free.

5The brand

Keep what people recognise. Show it with more confidence.

No need to start over. Just express it better.

  • ✓
    Keep the name, colours and logoThe navy, the teal and the CVBS monogram stay. That is the recognition you have earned.
  • ✓
    Lead with outcomesHours saved, better rates, less stress. "Free" becomes "paid by the venue, never by you."
  • ✓
    Sound like the trusted insiderCalm, expert, well-connected. The person every busy organiser wishes they had on speed dial.

What should someone feel in the first five seconds? "These are clearly the experts. I can stop worrying about this."

6The website's job

Treat the website as your best salesperson.

It works every hour, and it never forgets the pitch.

  • ✓
    Catch attentionA strong homepage and pages for each city, so people searching for venues find you first.
  • ✓
    Build trustClient stories, results and your team, so people feel safe choosing you.
  • ✓
    Make it easy to actA simple brief form and clear next step on every page, so interest turns into enquiries.
How to do it

Build it in stages. Launch the parts that bring in enquiries first; add the helpful extras later.

7Instagram

Stay front of mind with the people who send the briefs.

A simple, steady presence. Not a second job.

  • ✓
    Show beautiful venues and destinationsYour product is visual. The rooms and places you can access sell themselves.
  • ✓
    Share simple, helpful tipsQuick advice for event organisers builds trust and shows your expertise.
  • ✓
    Point everything back to the websiteWhen someone needs a venue, you are the name they already follow and trust.
Keep it light

A handful of posts a month is plenty. The tools in this plan make creating them almost effortless.

8Our philosophy

Technology should never replace relationships.

Your value has always come from people.

The relationships you have built with venue managers, hotel groups and suppliers over 35 years are what make CVBS different. Nothing in this plan changes that.

Technology has one job here: to quietly remove repetitive admin, organise your shared knowledge, and give the team more time for what they do best. Clients keep working directly with experienced consultants. Those consultants simply have better tools, faster answers and more time to focus on what matters most.

The reassurance

We are not changing the heart of CVBS. We are protecting it, strengthening it, and making it easier to grow.

9Working smarter behind the scenes

Three ways to strengthen CVBS from the inside.

None of this changes the service. It changes how easily you deliver it.

  • 1
    Protect 35 years of knowledgeEvery proposal, venue inspection, negotiation and preferred supplier, gathered into one place the whole team can search in seconds. The value isn't the technology. It's making sure that knowledge is never lost.
  • 2
    Give the team more time with clientsDrafting proposals, finding past events, repetitive emails, meeting notes and contracts, all quietly handled in the background. Clients receive exactly the same personal service. The consultants simply have more hours for where they are most valuable, close to a full extra person's worth of time each week, without hiring anyone.
  • 3
    A business that doesn't rely on Karen and AJ remembering everythingTheir decades of experience become an asset the whole team can draw on. Instead of asking Karen, a consultant simply searches one system:
"Have we used this venue before?"One search, instant answer.
"Who was the GM we dealt with in Cairns?"Found in seconds, ready before the call.
"What concessions did we negotiate for that client?"The full history, there when you need it.
The biggest opportunity

This turns decades of experience into a shared business asset: a company that is easier to run, less dependent on any one person, and ready to grow.

A simple path, one step at a time.

  • ✓
    Years 2–3 · SmarterHelpful tools for clients, and quick, accurate budgets from your own real numbers.
  • ✓
    Years 3–5 · The leaderKnown as the experts who simply know the market better than anyone else.
The destination

This was never a website redesign. It is the plan for the next decade of CVBS. The first step is simply to begin.
