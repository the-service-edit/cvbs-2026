Where CVBS is today

A

An elite service

Deep supplier relationships, real negotiating leverage, and a free-to-client model that removes the buyer's risk. The offer is genuinely top-tier.

B

Founder-dependent operations

Where CVBS could be

Australia's definitive conference and venue sourcing consultancy: premium in perception, instrumented with data, and operating with the leverage of a team twice its size.

The five moves that matter most

Move 01 · Reposition

Reframe CVBS from "venue finder" to strategic procurement advisor

Lead with savings, risk reduction and hours returned, not with "free worldwide venue research". The redesign concept already does this; it should be shipped, not shelved.

Move 02 · Convert

Replace the brochure site with a conversion engine

The redesign's 30-second brief, 48-hour promise and client proof are designed to turn anonymous traffic into qualified enquiries. That is a revenue change, not a cosmetic one.

Move 03 · Systematise

Turn 30 years of venue and rate knowledge into a searchable internal asset

An internal AI assistant over the venue database, past proposals and contracts removes the founder bottleneck and lets the team answer in seconds what currently takes hours.

Move 04 · Automate

Remove the busywork between enquiry and booking

Proposal drafting, follow-ups, CRM updates and reporting are repetitive and automatable. Reclaiming this time is reclaiming sellable capacity.

Move 05 · Compound

Build proprietary venue and pricing intelligence

Every brief CVBS handles is data no competitor has. Captured properly, it becomes benchmarking, predictive budgeting and a defensible moat.

The concept is a genuine repositioning. Now it has to earn the premium it promises.

Reviewed live at the-service-edit.github.io/cvbs-2026. This is a critique, not a compliment. The concept gets the strategy right; the notes below are what separates a strong redesign from a category-defining one.

What the redesign gets right

01

It sells the relationship, not the search

"A search engine returns six hundred rooms. We return three, and we've stood in all of them." This is the whole business in one line, and it reframes CVBS above every booking platform.

02

It makes a concrete promise

Free to you · shortlist in 48 hours · independent. Three specific, repeated proof points replace the old site's vague "we do the legwork".

03

A 30-second brief form sits in the hero. Intent is captured at peak interest instead of being routed to a buried quote page.

04

Named clients (Elders, Lend Lease, Energex, Medtronic), real quotes, and the directors' own faces and number. Trust is evidenced, not asserted.

05

It has a clear narrative spine

Hero → trust → process → services → people → proof → venues → act. A visitor is guided, not left to wander.

06

It reads as premium before a word is read

Restraint, space, a confident palette and considered type signal a high-end consultancy. Perception shifts in the first second.

Brand Evolution

Evolve the brand without spending the trust it has banked since 1989.

CVBS does not need reinvention. It needs articulation. The goal is to make 35 years of equity legible to a modern, premium buyer, without discarding the recognition that equity carries.

Positioning · the one move

From "free worldwide venue research" to "Australia's trusted conference and venue sourcing consultancy. We source what you cannot find, and negotiate what you cannot get, at no cost to you."

The word "consultancy" does heavy lifting. It moves CVBS out of the directory category, where it competes against free tools, and into the advisory category, where it competes on expertise, access and outcomes. Everything else in the brand should reinforce that single shift.

The brand system, evolved

A

Messaging

Lead with outcomes: hours saved, rates negotiated, risk removed. "Free" becomes "paid by the venue, never by you", a proof of independence rather than a discount.

Aa

Tone of voice

Understated concierge. Confident, precise, never loud. The voice of someone who has done this ten thousand times and does not need to oversell.

◆

Visual identity

Keep the CVBS monogram and gradient as the recognisable anchor. Modernise the system around it: space, restraint, and a clear hierarchy.

●

Colour evolution

Retain navy and teal as the equity colours. Add stone as a premium neutral and use teal sparingly, as an accent of confidence rather than a flood.

T

Typography

Inter for clarity and modernity, with tight tracking on headlines and a restrained serif for moments of editorial warmth. Type becomes a signal of craft.

▦

Photography

Real venues, real rooms, real people. Editorial, calm, aspirational. Never generic stock. The imagery should look like the events CVBS actually delivers.

＋

Iconography & graphic language

Fine-line, precise marks. A quiet system of eyebrows, rules and the short teal bar that reads as considered, not decorative.

◑

Brand personality

The trusted insider. Discreet, well-connected, unflappable. The person every executive assistant wishes they had on speed dial.

◇

Emotional positioning

Relief and reassurance. CVBS removes the single most stressful, time-consuming part of an event from a busy person's plate.

Differentiation · what only CVBS can say

Access, not search

Booking platforms list what is public. CVBS knows what is actually available, because a real person knows the general managers by name. That access cannot be commoditised.

Aligned, not conflicted

Paid by the venue, working for the client, independent of any one group. The model itself is the differentiator, and almost no competitor can claim it as cleanly.

The five-second feeling

Within five seconds of discovering CVBS, somebody should feel: "These people are the quiet experts. They are clearly the best at this, they will save me hours and money, and I can stop worrying about it."

Priority High Effort Articulation, not rebrand ROI Higher-value clients, premium fees, easier sales

Suggested visualA one-page brand-at-a-glance: monogram, palette swatches, type specimen, voice principles, and the five-second feeling as the headline.

Speaker notes

Reassure the directors that "evolution" means keeping what customers recognise, the name, the monogram, the navy and teal, and changing how confidently it is expressed. The risk is not changing too much; it is changing too little and remaining invisible to the premium buyer. The single sentence to leave them with is the positioning line: trusted consultancy, not free directory.

AI Transformation Strategy

The real prize is not a chatbot. It is turning 35 years of knowledge into leverage.

CVBS's competitive advantage is information: which venues, which rates, which relationships, which past deals. Today that lives in inboxes and two people's memories. AI's job is to make it instantly retrievable, and to remove the repetitive work that stops the team from selling. This is the largest opportunity in this document.

The principle

AI should not replace the relationships that make CVBS valuable. It should remove everything around them, so the team spends its hours on negotiation and advice, not admin and search.
